A communications device is any piece of hardware that can transmit data, instructions, and information between a sending and receiving device. A modem is a type of communications device that connects a communications channel to a sending or receiving device like a computer. Data is processed as digital signals by computers. Depending on the communications channel, data, instructions, and information are transmitted in either analog or digital form. A continuous electrical wave constitutes an analog signal. A digital signal is made up of individual electrical pulses that represent bits that are grouped together to form bytes.

A storage unit is a component of a computer system that stores the information and instructions to be processed. A storage device is a component of computer hardware that stores information/data in order to process the results of any computational work. A computer cannot run or even boot up without a storage device. In other words, a storage device is hardware that is used for storing, transferring, or extracting data files. It can also temporarily and permanently store information/data.


When a computer receives data from an input device (such as a keyboard), it passes through an intermediate stage before being sent to an output device (e.g., monitor).
